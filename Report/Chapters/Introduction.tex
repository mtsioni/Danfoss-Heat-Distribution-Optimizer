\section{Problem Statement}
Manual scheduling of heat generation and its distribution is very inefficient and costly. The utility company
\textit{Danfoss} is currently seeking a heat distribution optimization tool, that would automize
the heat distribution effectively, for the lowest price possible, with biggest possible
profit from electricity generation, in two distinct scenarios, and for two distinct time periods, while also
implementing scheduling maintenance for the units.

\subsection{Background}
\textit{Danfoss} company is facing a significant challenge with scheduling the heat distribution among
approximately 1600 buildings in the city \textit{Heatington} via single district heating network, taking
two different scenarios and two different periods into consideration when scheduling.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent There are six available production units in total, namely: \textbf{Gas Boiler 1}, \textbf{Gas Boiler 2}, \textbf{Gas Boiler 3},
\textbf{Oil Boiler 1}, \textbf{Gas Motor 1}, and \textbf{Electric Boiler 1}. Each scenario has different combination of the units available.
The first scenario is as follows: heat is distributed across one heating area, but only three gas boilers
and one oil boiler are available from all the production units. In the second scenario the heat is also distributed
across the same singular heating area, but now there are only two gas boilers, one gas motor, and one electric boiler
available from all the production units. As for the periods, there is the Summer period and the Winter period, where both
of these periods have specific values per each full hour for what the heat demand is, and what is the electricity cost
at that time. The Summer period starts on 8\textsuperscript{th} of September 2025 at 00:00 and ends on 21\textsuperscript{st}
of September at midnight, and the winter period starts on 5\textsuperscript{th} of January 2026 at 00:00 and ends on
18\textsuperscript{th} of January at midnight.

\vspace{1cm}

\noindent The company has been shceduling the distribution manually. They have to compare every aspect of all the
production units, that depends also on the scenario and season, such as: the maximal heat that the unit
can produce, the cost of producing heat using this unit, the CO\textsubscript{2} emissions produced by
the unit, the amount of fuel needed for generating the heat, and the maximal amount of electricity that
could be produced. There is also a need to check all of the information for each period and do manual calculations
to figure out what production unit should be used at a particular time, according to the objective of keeping the
expenses as low as possible, while also seeking the highest possible gained revenue. One of the challenges that they
run into when executing the calculations is the cost of electricity, since its value is changing into a new,
completely different value every hour. Another challenge is the maintenance planning, which done manually takes
great amount of time when calculating and scheduling the heat distribution. The maintenance has to be set, ranging from
30 to 60 hours, for one chosen production unit in each scenario once per year, preferably in the summer period,
since the heat demand is lower in summer.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent All of this resulted in being inefficient, as the manual scheduling takes an overwhelming amount of time. It also
leads to the company having more expenses than needed for distributing the heat due to insufficient calculations,
to missed opportunity of a possible greater revenue gained for the electricity produced, and finally,
this system also creates more space for error made or influenced by human factor.

\subsection{Relevance}
Automizing and optimizing the scheduling of the heat distribution using the production units available
will result in reducing the expenses needed, and also in boosting the revenue gained from electricity produced.
Not only will it save the company's money for heat generation and distribution, and increase the profit
from electricity generation, but it will also make the process more efficient and it will eliminate the
possibility of error made by a human factor.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent Also, it is possible to design and engineer the system in a way, where it could be reused for either another
city, or another district heating area. It could also be reused for if there are new production units added,
production units totally removed, or if the data and values for the existing production units parameters were to be changed.
Ensuring such scalability for this project would make it a very efficient and reliable tool that can be used by the company for years.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent One of the main problem is calculating the net production cost. Every production unit has a fixed price for producing
1 MWh of heat. However, the cost for some units that are using or producing electricity, the \textbf{Gas Motor 1} and \textbf{Electric Boiler 1},
can be different from the fixed price. The \textbf{Gas Motor 1} produces electricity as a byproduct that can be sold, which
reduces its production cost. The \textbf{Electric Boiler 1} works in an opposite way by being dependent on the electricity
to run, meaning the higher the electricity price is the more expensive it is to use the production unit. The \textbf{Optimizer} uses
these net production costs to rank each unit from cheapest to the most expensive for each hour.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent There is also a scenario where the electricity prices have a negative value. This event flips the logic around,
where the \textbf{Gas Motor 1} now has to paid for to produce the electricity, making it one of the most expensive units
to run, and the \textbf{Electric Boiler 1} now gets paid for consuming the electricity, making it potentially the cheapest,
or maybe even profitable option.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent Furthermore, developing the \textbf{Data Visualization} module within the system will help the operators
and the schedulers to view clear diagram and graph, turning complex optimization result data into intuitive and easily
understandable format. The module will display key information, such as the heat demand, the heat production, the amount
of electricity used, the electricity price, the CO\textsubscript{2} emissions produced, the fuel consumption, and the production cost.
The graph will function in an intelligent way, where some of the information that is special for certain scenario,
for example the amount of electricity used and the electricity price, will only be available for viewing when the corresponding scenario is selected.
The graph will be interactable in such way, where the operator is able to click into specific position in the graph, to view the
information connected with the production unit, for example the date and time of the current selected point on graph, how much heat
was generated by this unit, etc.

\section{Goal and Objectives}
The goal of this project is to create a system for the utility company \textit{Danfoss},
which is responsible for heat generation and distribution to approximately 1600 buildings in 
the city \textit{Heatington}, using a single district heating system. The system must be capable of operating
in two distinct scenarios where there are different production units available, and also must be able to operate
throughout two different 14 days long periods, in summer and in winter. The automatization and optimized scheduling
of production units is going to help with delivering sufficient amount of heat according to the heat demand at the
lowest possible cost, and it will maximize the possible profit to be gained from the electricty generation.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent The objectives for creation of this project are:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Develop a modular software system consisting of 5 different modules, namely:
        \begin{enumerate}
            \item \textbf{Asset Manager (AM)} - acting as a source repository for static system information,
            responsible for retrieving the data when called by the \textbf{Optimizer} module or the \textbf{Data Visualization} module
            \item \textbf{Source Data Manager (SDM)} - acting as a source repository for dynamic system information,
            responsible for retrieving the data when called by the \textbf{Optimizer} module or the \textbf{Data Visualization} module
            \item \textbf{Result Data Manager (RDM)} - acting as a sink repository for optimization results,
            responsible for saving the optimized data from the \textbf{Optimizer} module into CSV files for each unit, and retrieving that data
            when called by the \textbf{Data Visualization} module
            \item \textbf{Optimizer (OPT)} - a kernel module for optimization of the energy production
            \item \textbf{Data Visualization (DV)} - responsible for visualizing the optimized result data via diagrams
            and charts on the GUI (Graphical User Interface) of the software
        \end{enumerate}
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Develop logic for the \textbf{Optimizer} module, in a way where it optimizes the heat production and distribution schedule
    with the lowest expenses possible, and with the highest profit gained possible
    \item Design the system in a way where it will operate in two distinct scenarios and two different time periods,
    that being the summer period and the winter period
    \item Design the \textbf{Result Data Manager} so it saves all of the optimized result data into CSV files with the name identical
    to the production unit name
    \item Create a GUI for visualizing the optimized result data and the information about each unit using the Avalonia module
    \item Implement a control panel for the scenario and the period switching into the GUI
    \item Implement a maintenance control panel for assigning the maintenance period into the GUI
    \item Implement the possibility to switch between dark and light theme for a better user experience
\end{itemize}
